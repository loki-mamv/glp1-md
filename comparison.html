GLP-1 Comparison Guide

Updated Feb 2026 · 15 min read

A head-to-head comparison of the major GLP-1 medications: Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, and Zepbound. Understand the differences in efficacy, side effects, cost, and which might be right for you.

Quick Summary

  • Best for diabetes (FDA-approved): Ozempic, Mounjaro
  • Best for weight loss (FDA-approved): Wegovy, Zepbound
  • Highest average weight loss: Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) at ~21%
  • Oral option available: Rybelsus (semaglutide pill)

Understanding the Medications

Ozempic (Semaglutide)

0.25mg → 0.5mg → 1mg → 2mg

The most widely prescribed GLP-1, FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes. While not officially approved for weight loss, significant weight reduction is a well-documented benefit. Extensive safety data from years of use.

Wegovy (Semaglutide 2.4mg)

0.25mg → 0.5mg → 1mg → 1.7mg → 2.4mg

Same molecule as Ozempic but dosed higher and FDA-approved specifically for chronic weight management. Indicated for adults with BMI ≥30 or BMI ≥27 with weight-related conditions.

Mounjaro (Tirzepatide)

2.5mg → 5mg → 7.5mg → 10mg → 12.5mg → 15mg

Dual GIP/GLP-1 agonist that showed superior weight loss in clinical trials. FDA-approved for type 2 diabetes. The dual mechanism may explain its enhanced efficacy compared to GLP-1-only drugs.

Zepbound (Tirzepatide)

2.5mg → 5mg → 7.5mg → 10mg → 12.5mg → 15mg

Same molecule as Mounjaro, FDA-approved for weight management. In the SURMOUNT trials, participants lost an average of 21% of body weight—the highest of any approved medication.

Efficacy Comparison

Weight Loss Results

Clinical trial data shows tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) produces greater weight loss on average:

  • STEP trials (Wegovy): 16-17% average weight loss at 68 weeks
  • SURMOUNT trials (Zepbound): 21% average weight loss at 72 weeks
  • SURPASS trials (Mounjaro): 15-21% weight loss (dose-dependent) plus superior A1C reduction

⚠️ Individual Results Vary

These are average results from clinical trials. Your results may be higher or lower depending on diet, exercise, dosing, and individual metabolism. Some people are "super responders" while others have more modest results.

Diabetes Control

For A1C reduction in type 2 diabetes:

  • Mounjaro shows the greatest A1C reduction (2-2.5%)
  • Ozempic provides excellent glycemic control (1.5-2%)
  • Both medications also reduce cardiovascular risk

Side Effects Comparison

All GLP-1 medications share similar side effect profiles. The most common are gastrointestinal:

  • Nausea — Most common, usually improves over time
  • Vomiting — More common during dose escalation
  • Diarrhea
  • Constipation
  • Injection site reactions

Tirzepatide may cause slightly more GI side effects initially, but this often reflects its greater potency. Slow titration helps minimize symptoms.

Cost & Insurance

Without insurance, all these medications are expensive ($900-$1,400/month). Coverage varies significantly:

  • Diabetes medications (Ozempic, Mounjaro) — Generally better coverage under pharmacy benefits
  • Weight loss medications (Wegovy, Zepbound) — Often excluded or require prior authorization
  • Manufacturer savings cards — Can reduce cost to $25-$500/month for commercially insured patients

Which One Should You Choose?

Consider Ozempic or Mounjaro if:

  • You have type 2 diabetes (FDA-approved indication)
  • Your insurance covers diabetes medications but not weight loss drugs
  • You want established safety data (Ozempic has longer track record)

Consider Wegovy or Zepbound if:

  • Weight loss is your primary goal (not diabetes)
  • Your insurance covers anti-obesity medications
  • You want the FDA-approved indication for weight management

Consider Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) if:

  • You want maximum weight loss potential
  • You haven't responded well to semaglutide
  • You have both diabetes and significant weight to lose

Talk to Your Doctor

The best medication depends on your individual health situation, insurance coverage, and goals. This guide is for information only.
