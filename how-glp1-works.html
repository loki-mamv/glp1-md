How GLP-1 Medications Work

Updated Feb 2026 ¬∑ 12 min read

Understanding the science behind medications like Ozempic, Wegovy, and Mounjaro. How they affect your appetite, blood sugar, and metabolism to produce weight loss.

Key Takeaways

  • GLP-1 medications mimic a natural hormone your body produces after eating
  • They work on multiple systems: brain (appetite), pancreas (insulin), and stomach (digestion)
  • Effects include reduced hunger, earlier fullness, and better blood sugar control
  • Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound) activates both GLP-1 and GIP receptors

What is GLP-1?

GLP-1 (glucagon-like peptide-1) is a hormone naturally produced by your intestines after you eat. It's called an "incretin" hormone because it helps regulate blood sugar and appetite. Natural GLP-1 is rapidly broken down in the body‚Äîlasting only a few minutes.

GLP-1 medications like semaglutide and tirzepatide are modified versions that resist breakdown, allowing them to stay active for about a week. This sustained activity produces more powerful effects than your natural GLP-1.

Semaglutide vs Tirzepatide

Semaglutide (Ozempic, Wegovy)

Semaglutide is a pure GLP-1 receptor agonist. It binds to and activates GLP-1 receptors throughout the body. It's been used since 2017 and has extensive safety data.

Tirzepatide (Mounjaro, Zepbound)

Tirzepatide is a "dual agonist" that activates both GLP-1 and GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic polypeptide) receptors. GIP is another incretin hormone with complementary effects.

The dual mechanism may explain tirzepatide's superior weight loss in clinical trials. GIP receptors in fat tissue and the brain may provide additional metabolic benefits beyond GLP-1 alone.

Why Two Hormones May Be Better

  • GIP enhances GLP-1's effects on appetite and insulin
  • GIP receptors in fat tissue may improve fat metabolism
  • Dual activation may reduce some GI side effects
  • Clinical trials show ~5% more weight loss vs semaglutide

Why Do These Medications Work So Well?

Traditional weight loss advice‚Äîeat less, move more‚Äîfights against powerful biological systems designed to prevent starvation. When you lose weight through diet alone, your body fights back:

  • Hunger hormones increase
  • Metabolic rate decreases
  • Food becomes more rewarding to your brain

GLP-1 medications work with your biology rather than against it. By reducing appetite signals and improving metabolic hormones, they make sustainable calorie reduction feel natural rather than forced.

The Importance of Slow Titration

These medications are started at low doses and gradually increased. This titration period serves several purposes:

  • Allows your body to adjust, minimizing side effects
  • Lets GLP-1 receptors adapt to continuous stimulation
  • Helps find your effective dose (not everyone needs maximum dose)
  • Reduces nausea and GI symptoms during adjustment

What the Research Shows

Clinical trials have demonstrated consistent results:

  • STEP trials (semaglutide): 15-17% average weight loss, maintained over 2+ years with continued use
  • SURMOUNT trials (tirzepatide): 21% average weight loss, with some patients losing over 25%
  • SELECT trial: 20% reduction in cardiovascular events for people with obesity

Importantly, weight tends to return when medications are stopped‚Äîsimilar to how blood pressure rises when you stop blood pressure medication. This suggests these drugs treat the underlying biology of obesity rather than providing a temporary fix.

Current Research

Scientists are studying GLP-1 medications for many other conditions:

  • Non-alcoholic fatty liver disease (NAFLD/NASH)
  • Alzheimer's disease and cognitive decline
  • Chronic kidney disease
  • Addiction and substance use disorders
  • Sleep apnea

The wide distribution of GLP-1 receptors throughout the body means these medications may have benefits we're only beginning to understand.
