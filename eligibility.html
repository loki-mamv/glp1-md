Eligibility Criteria

Who Qualifies for GLP-1 Medications?

FDA-approved indications and eligibility criteria for GLP-1 receptor agonists. Different medications are approved for different conditions.

For Type 2 Diabetes

GLP-1 medications for diabetes (Ozempic, Mounjaro, Trulicity, Rybelsus, Victoza) are FDA-approved for adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus.

Diabetes Indication

Eligible if you have:

  • Diagnosis of type 2 diabetes mellitus
  • Need for improved blood sugar (glycemic) control
  • Currently using or have tried diet and exercise modifications
  • May be used alone or with other diabetes medications

Typical treatment pathway: GLP-1 medications are usually not first-line therapy. Most insurance plans require trying metformin first unless contraindicated. Your doctor will document why a GLP-1 is appropriate for you.

Additional Cardiovascular Indication

Ozempic (semaglutide) and some other GLP-1s are also approved to reduce the risk of major cardiovascular events (heart attack, stroke, cardiovascular death) in adults with type 2 diabetes and established cardiovascular disease.

Adolescent Eligibility (Wegovy Only)

Wegovy is approved for adolescents aged 12 and older with:

  • Initial BMI at the 95th percentile or higher for their age and sex
  • Body weight above 60 kg (132 lbs)

Getting Started: What to Expect

1. Consultation with Your Doctor

Your healthcare provider will:

  • Review your medical history and current medications
  • Confirm you meet the eligibility criteria
  • Discuss benefits, risks, and realistic expectations
  • Check for contraindications
  • Order baseline labs (A1C for diabetes, thyroid function if indicated)

2. Insurance Prior Authorization

Most insurance plans require prior authorization. Your doctor's office will typically handle this, documenting:

  • Your diagnosis (diabetes or obesity with BMI criteria)
  • Previous treatments tried (e.g., metformin for diabetes, diet/exercise for weight loss)
  • Medical necessity for the specific medication

3. Starting Treatment

GLP-1 medications start at a low dose and increase gradually (titration) to minimize side effects:

  • Ozempic: Start 0.25 mg weekly ‚Üí increase every 4 weeks
  • Wegovy: Start 0.25 mg weekly ‚Üí titrate over 16-20 weeks to 2.4 mg
  • Mounjaro/Zepbound: Start 2.5 mg weekly ‚Üí increase every 4 weeks

Frequently Asked Questions

Can I get GLP-1 medications if I'm just slightly overweight?

For weight loss medications (Wegovy, Zepbound), you need either BMI ‚â•30, or BMI ‚â•27 with a weight-related health condition. If you don't meet these criteria, the medications are not FDA-approved for you, and insurance won't cover them.

What if I have type 2 diabetes AND want to lose weight?

If you have type 2 diabetes, you may qualify for Ozempic or Mounjaro (diabetes indications) which also produce significant weight loss. Insurance coverage is typically better for diabetes indications.

Do I need to have tried other weight loss methods first?

The FDA approval doesn't require previous failed attempts, but most insurance plans do require documentation that you've tried diet and exercise modifications. Some may require trying other medications first (step therapy).

Can I use GLP-1 medications long-term?

Yes, these are chronic condition medications intended for long-term use. Studies show weight regain typically occurs if the medication is stopped. Discuss long-term plans with your doctor.
