Getting Insurance Coverage

Diabetes Medications (Ozempic, Mounjaro)

If you have type 2 diabetes, coverage is generally better:

  • These are FDA-approved for diabetes management
  • Most commercial plans cover them under pharmacy benefits
  • May require prior authorization or step therapy
  • Medicare Part D typically covers for diabetes indication

Weight Loss Medications (Wegovy, Zepbound)

Coverage for obesity medications is more challenging:

  • Many plans exclude "weight loss drugs" entirely
  • Some employers are adding coverage due to demand
  • Prior authorization almost always required
  • Medicare generally does NOT cover weight loss medications

üí° Prior Authorization Tips

  • Document your BMI and weight-related conditions
  • Have your doctor note previous weight loss attempts
  • Include any obesity-related comorbidities (sleep apnea, joint pain, prediabetes)
  • Ask if your plan requires "step therapy" (trying other meds first)
  • Appeal if initially denied‚Äîmany denials are overturned

Options If You Don't Have Coverage

1. Eli Lilly's LillyDirect

Zepbound via Telehealth

Lilly offers Zepbound through its direct-to-patient program with lower cash prices: $399/month for lower doses, $549/month for higher doses. Includes telehealth consultation.

2. Patient Assistance Programs

For Uninsured/Underinsured

Both manufacturers offer patient assistance for those who qualify based on income. Contact Novo Nordisk or Eli Lilly patient assistance programs directly.

3. Compounding Pharmacies

Compounded Semaglutide/Tirzepatide

Due to FDA-recognized shortages, compounding pharmacies can legally produce these medications at lower cost ($200-400/month). Quality varies‚Äîuse reputable 503B pharmacies only.

4. Check Multiple Pharmacies

Price Shopping

Prices vary significantly between pharmacies. Use GoodRx or RxSaver to compare. Some warehouse clubs (Costco, Sam's) have competitive pharmacy pricing.

Questions to Ask Your Insurance

  1. Is [medication name] covered under my plan?
  2. Which tier is it on? What's my copay?
  3. Is prior authorization required?
  4. Are there step therapy requirements?
  5. Is there a quantity limit?
  6. Does my plan have a specialty pharmacy requirement?

The Medicare Gap

Medicare Part D coverage for these medications depends on indication:

  • For diabetes (Ozempic, Mounjaro): Generally covered, though may be on higher tiers
  • For weight loss (Wegovy, Zepbound): NOT covered‚ÄîMedicare explicitly excludes drugs for weight loss

Legislation has been proposed to change this, but as of 2026, weight loss medications remain excluded from Medicare coverage.

Is the Cost Worth It?

Consider the full picture:

  • Health improvements: Better A1C, blood pressure, reduced sleep apnea, joint pain relief
  • Reduced other costs: Potentially fewer medications for related conditions
  • Quality of life: Many patients report dramatic improvements in daily functioning
  • Long-term savings: Preventing diabetes complications, joint replacements, heart disease

Work with your healthcare team to find the most cost-effective option that works for your situation.
